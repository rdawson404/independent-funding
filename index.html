Capital Solutions

Programs designed for real businesses

Every program has a right fit and a wrong fit. We're upfront about both.

Our Commitment: We match you to the right program for your situation — and we're transparent about costs, terms, and expectations from day one.
📈
SBA & Long-Term Options
Bankable · 5–25 Years
Conventional-rate, long-term financing through SBA programs or bank partners. The gold standard — the lower-cost capital every growing business should aim for. (Referral product — placed through lending partners)
Best For
  • 680+ FICO with clean history
  • 2+ years in business
  • Stable, documented revenue
Not Yet If
  • Active MCAs or heavy stacking
  • Credit or banking issues unresolved
🏦
Secured Lines of Credit
Long-Term · Revolving
Asset-backed revolving credit — draw what you need, repay, draw again. One of the lowest cost structures available outside of conventional bank financing.
Best For
  • Businesses with 680+ FICO
  • Real estate or equipment assets
  • Companies ready to move beyond short-term capital
Consider Carefully If
  • Collateral is limited or encumbered
📅
Monthly Payment Programs
Stability · 12–36 Months
Lower-frequency payment structure with reduced factor rates. Ideal for businesses with consistent monthly revenue and stronger credit profiles looking to protect daily cash flow.
Best For
  • Established businesses with 650+ FICO
  • Businesses that operate on monthly cycles
  • Companies transitioning from daily programs
Consider Carefully If
  • Significant negative days in statements
  • Heavy existing MCA stacking
🔄
Revenue-Based Financing
Flexibility · Ongoing
Payments flex with your revenue — busier months pay more, slower months pay less. Designed to reduce the strain of fixed payments during natural business cycles.
Best For
  • Seasonal or cyclical businesses
  • Service-based companies
  • Businesses with variable monthly revenue
Consider Carefully If
  • Revenue is already on a downward trend
🔗
Consolidation & Restructure
Relief · Strategic
Combine multiple active positions into a single, manageable payment. Reduce daily cash drain, improve your approval ceiling, and create breathing room for growth.
Best For
  • Businesses with 2+ active positions
  • Companies overwhelmed by daily debits
  • Owners building toward longer-term financing
Not Always Available If
  • Revenue has declined significantly
  • Defaults or UCC liens are present
⚡
Short-Term Working Capital
Speed · 3–12 Months
Revenue-based advance structured against daily or weekly deposits. The fastest access to capital — typically 24 hours from approval to funding.
Best For
  • Immediate operational gaps
  • Seasonal inventory needs
  • Time-sensitive opportunities
  • Businesses with strong daily revenue
Consider Carefully If
  • Cash flow is already tight
  • You are already 50%+ over-leveraged

Qualification Overview

What you can qualify for — at every level

Credit is one factor. Revenue, deposits, and existing obligations matter just as much. Here's an honest breakdown.

A

Tier A — Prime

680–800 FICO · Strong Revenue · Clean History
Monthly payment programs — 8–14% conventional
Secured line of credit options
SBA pathway (with qualifying revenue)
Lowest factor rates available
Highest approval ceilings
The strongest position for long-term financing. Consistent deposit patterns are key to unlocking the best rates.
B

Tier B — Near-Prime

600–679 FICO · Moderate Revenue
May qualify for monthly payment programs
Factor rates starting from 1.17
Hybrid bi-weekly structures available
Consolidation structures may apply
Strong deposits and consistent revenue can offset credit challenges. Our underwriting weighs cash flow heavily at this tier.
C

Tier C — Cash Flow Based

Below 600 FICO · Deposit-Driven Approval
Weekly payment programs only
Daily payment programs
Approval based primarily on deposit volume and frequency
No monthly programs available at this tier
We structure carefully at this level — only what your cash flow can genuinely support. We'll decline if the deal doesn't make sense for your business.
Important: Credit score is one data point — not the whole picture. Tier A starts at 680, Tier B covers 600–679, and Tier C applies below 600. Final qualification also depends on average monthly revenue, deposit frequency, number of negative days, and existing exposure. A 670 FICO with poor deposit patterns may qualify for less than a 610 FICO with strong, consistent deposits. Our underwriting analyzes all of these factors before presenting options.

The Full Picture

What our underwriting evaluates

📊
Average Monthly Revenue
The foundation of every underwriting decision. We typically structure funding at 75–150% of monthly revenue depending on tier and program type.
📅
Deposit Frequency
How often money comes in matters as much as how much. Consistent daily deposits signal a healthier business than sporadic large ones.
❌
Negative Days
Days with a negative balance are a significant concern. More than 5–7 per month substantially reduces available options and approval ceiling.
🏋️
Existing Obligations
Active positions reduce approval ceilings. Heavy stacking is one of the fastest ways to limit your funding options and increase risk.
💳
FICO & Credit History
Personal credit affects rate and program access — but strong revenue and deposit patterns can significantly offset lower scores.
📐
Approval Ceiling
The maximum we'll extend based on all factors combined. We calculate this before structuring — so you're never over-leveraged from day one.

Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers to common questions

No jargon, no fine print. Here's what you need to know about working with Independent Funding.

What makes Independent Funding different?
We combine fast access to capital with complete transparency and dedicated support. You'll always know the full cost of your deal before signing, and you'll have a dedicated team throughout your entire term — not a revolving door of contacts.
How fast can I get funded?
Most businesses receive a decision the same day they apply. Once approved and signed, funds are typically deposited within 24 hours. Our streamlined process is designed to respect your time and urgency.
What does it cost? Are there hidden fees?
No hidden fees — ever. We show you the factor rate, total payback amount, and payment schedule upfront before you sign anything. The rate we quote is the rate you pay. We believe transparency builds trust, and trust builds lasting partnerships.
What do I need to apply?
A simple application and your most recent 3 months of bank statements. That's it to get started. No hard credit pull at application. We may request additional documentation depending on the program and amount, but we'll always be upfront about what's needed.
What if I have existing funding positions?
We work with businesses that have existing positions regularly. Depending on your revenue, deposit patterns, and current exposure, we may be able to consolidate positions into a single payment — or structure new funding alongside existing advances. We'll review your full picture and give you honest options.
Can you help me transition to longer-term financing?
Absolutely. Many of our clients start with short-term capital and work toward monthly payment programs, secured lines of credit, or SBA financing. We help you build a path from where you are to where you want to be — and we're upfront about what it takes to get there.
What are the minimum qualifications?
Generally, we look for at least 6 months in business, $15,000+ in monthly revenue, and an active business bank account. Credit score is a factor but not the only one — strong deposits and consistent revenue can offset lower scores. Apply and we'll tell you exactly where you stand.
Who do I contact after I'm funded?
Your dedicated account team. Unlike many funding companies, we don't disappear after the deal closes. You'll have direct access to the people managing your account for questions, renewals, or anything else that comes up. One call, one team.

Have a question we didn't cover? We'll give you a straight answer.

IMPORTANT DISCLOSURES

Independent Funding LLC provides merchant cash advances (MCAs) and revenue-based financing — these are commercial transactions, not loans. As such, they do not carry an Annual Percentage Rate (APR) and are not subject to state lending license requirements.

Pricing & Cost Structure:

  • Factor rates typically range from 1.17 to 1.49 depending on qualification tier and risk profile
  • Example: A $10,000 advance at a 1.30 factor rate = $13,000 total payback ($3,000 in total fees)
  • Terms range from 3 to 36 months depending on program
  • SBA and conventional loan programs (offered through bank partners) range from 5 to 25 years with conventional interest rates of 8–14%
  • No application fees, no hidden charges — full cost breakdown provided before signing

All transactions are subject to underwriting review and approval. Not all applicants will qualify.
